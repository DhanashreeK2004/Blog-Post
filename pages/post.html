Book Review of Hooked by Nir Eyal

Nir Eyal’s Hooked: How to Build Habit-Forming Products is a thought-provoking exploration of the psychology behind why people become deeply engaged with certain products and services. The book introduces the “Hook Model,” a four-step process—trigger, action, variable reward, and investment—that explains how successful products subtly influence user behavior and create lasting habits. Eyal uses real-world examples from apps and platforms we use daily, showing how these companies design experiences that keep users coming back without conscious effort. What makes the book particularly compelling is its balance between practical insights and ethical considerations, as it encourages creators to build products that genuinely improve users’ lives rather than exploit their attention. For designers, developers, and marketers, Hooked offers valuable lessons on user engagement, while for general readers, it provides a deeper awareness of how modern technology shapes our habits. Overall, it is an insightful and relevant read in today’s digital age, making us reflect not only on how products are built, but also on how we interact with them.
